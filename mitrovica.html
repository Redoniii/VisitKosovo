Isa Bey Mosque

Isa Bey Mosque is a historical mosque located in Mitrovica, Kosovo. It is one of the oldest and most significant Islamic cultural landmarks in the city. The mosque was built in the 15th century and is considered an important example of Ottoman-era architecture, with its distinctive domed roof and intricate tile work.

Artificial lake of Mitrovica

The artificial lake of Mitrovica is a man-made body of water in the city, created for recreational and aesthetic purposes. It offers a scenic location for activities such as boating, fishing, and picnicking, and serves as a popular destination for local residents and tourists alike. The lake creates a tranquil environment for visitors to enjoy.

"Trepca"

Mitrovica is home to the Trepca mine, one of the largest lead and zinc mines in Europe, and a significant source of the country's economy. The mine has a rich history dating back to the Roman Empire and has played a significant role in the industrial development of the region.

The Mazhiqi Mosque

The Mazhiqi Mosque (also known as the Mosque of Mujezin Hoxha) is a very old mosque which was built around the 14th century. It is located 13 km (8 mi) from Mitrovica, in the village of Mazhiq. The mosque is almost fully destroyed, and has lost its original function. It was built during the rule of the Ottoman Empire.

St. Peter's Basilica Church

St. Peter's Basilica Church, also known as Saxon's Church and Latin Church, dates to the 13th century. It was built for the Saxon miners and Catholic merchants and was used until the 16th century. The remains of the church are located in the old city of Stari Terg. St. Peter's Church, a three nave basilica, is of particular interest because of its gothic architectural style and construction techniques, as well as for the Byzantine mural paintings, which decorate the interior

Isa Boletini towers

The two towers are located in Boletin village. The first tower (men's tower) was built in 1887 by the father of Isa Boletini, Adem Boletini. It was destroyed in 1899 by the Ottoman invaders because Isa had built the first Albanian school on the region. Most of the battles happened in this tower. The other tower (women's tower) was built in 1912 and is now called the tower of the fire. It was destroyed during the war. The tower is now used as a museum.
